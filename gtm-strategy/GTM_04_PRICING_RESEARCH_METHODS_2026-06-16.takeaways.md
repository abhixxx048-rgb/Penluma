**This document explains how to find the right monthly price for Print-Flow-360 — not by guessing, but by asking real print shop owners a few simple questions and reading the answers correctly. Getting the price right early saves a lot of pain later: too low and you leave money behind, too high and owners walk away before you can even talk to them.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Van Westendorp survey** — A four-question survey you send to shop owners. You ask: "At what price is it so cheap you'd doubt it works? At what price is it a bargain? At what price does it start to feel expensive? At what price would you refuse to buy at all?" The answers plot out a safe price range — roughly $76 to $102 per month in the worked example. Only 30–50 responses needed to get a useful read.

- **Gabor-Granger survey** — A follow-up survey that tells you which price inside that range makes you the most money overall. You show each person one price and ask if they'd subscribe; then step up or down based on their answer. From about 80 responses you can see that $99/month earns more total revenue than $79 or $129. This is the number to build your main plan around.

- **Conjoint analysis** — A more powerful (and expensive) research method that tests different bundles of features and prices together. The document says: skip this for now. You need 200+ responses and a settled feature list. Come back to it once you have 50+ paying customers.

- **Fake-door price page** — The cheapest test you can run right now, before any survey. Put up a real pricing page with three price options and a "Start trial" button. Watch which price people actually click. A real click beats ten survey answers.

- **Sales-call anchoring** — In every founder sales call, ask the shop owner what their manual quoting takes in time and money each week, then show how $99/month is a fraction of that cost. Log their reaction. Ten honest conversations give you more signal than a 100-person survey of strangers.

- **Value-based pricing** — Price based on what Print-Flow-360 saves or earns for the shop owner, not on your server costs. A shop owner saving 6 hours a week of phone quoting at $22/hour saves roughly $570/month. A $99/month price is easy to justify when you frame it that way.

- **Per-store pricing (the recommended model)** — Charge per shop location, not per staff member and not per order. Shop owners think in terms of "I have one shop" — this model matches how they think and makes upgrades (adding a second store) feel natural.

- **Three-tier structure** — Solo at $49 (entry level, limited features), Growth at $99 (main plan, full studio — the sweet spot the research points to), and Multi-store/B2B at $199 (for corporate buyers who care about capability, not price).

**What to do with this:** Run the fake-door price page and 10 sales-call anchoring tests this week — zero cost, highest signal. Then send the Van Westendorp survey to 30+ shop owners to confirm the price range. Use Gabor-Granger on top to lock in $99 as your main tier price. Do not spend time or money on conjoint research yet.
