**This is a short English writing course made for you. It focuses on the exact mistakes you make in your everyday work writing — things like spelling errors, missing punctuation, and texting habits that slip into professional messages. Fixing these small, repeated slips will make your commits, messages, and pull requests much easier for others to read and trust.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Your top 7 mistakes** — The course starts by naming the exact errors you make most often, with a real before-and-after example. Knowing your specific weak spots means you study what you actually need, not things you already get right.
- **Articles (a, an, the)** — These tiny words trip up almost every non-native speaker. This lesson tells you when to use each one, and when to use none at all.
- **Much vs many / countable words** — "So much mistake" should be "so many mistakes." This lesson explains why — some words can be counted (mistakes, files, orders) and some cannot (water, work), and that changes which word you use.
- **Subject–verb agreement** — Making the verb match who or what is doing the action. "He run" is wrong; "He runs" is right. A quick rule set that fixes this for good.
- **Verb tenses** — Past, present, and future — and how to stop switching between them in the same sentence without realising it.
- **Plurals and possessives** — When to add *s*, when to add *'s*, and the classic trap: "its" (belonging to it) vs "it's" (it is).
- **Prepositions (in, on, at, for, with)** — The small linking words that native speakers use without thinking. This lesson gives you the most common patterns.
- **Punctuation and capitalization** — Full stops, question marks, commas — and the rule that "I" is always a capital letter, no exceptions.
- **Sentence structure** — How to break one long run-on sentence into clear, separate ideas so readers do not lose the thread.
- **Commonly confused words** — their / there / they're, your / you're, then / than. One lesson to sort them out once and for all.
- **Professional writing for developers** — How to write commits, pull requests, Slack messages, and code comments without texting habits (no "u", "r", "gonna"). Work writing has its own standard.
- **Spelling and typos** — The words engineers misspell most, plus how to let spell-check tools catch the rest automatically.
- **Master review (Lesson 12)** — One big practice drill that covers everything from all eleven lessons together. Use it to check how much has stuck.

**What to do with this:** Study one lesson at a time, spread over four weeks — about 20 to 30 minutes a day, a few days a week. Always try the practice drills yourself before checking the answers. A wrong answer you then correct teaches you far more than one you just read.
