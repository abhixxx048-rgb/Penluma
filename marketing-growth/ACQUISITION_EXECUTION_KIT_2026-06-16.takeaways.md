**This document is a step-by-step action plan for getting your first paying customers. It tells you exactly what to do each week — who to contact, what to say, and what to build online — so you are not guessing. It costs almost no money and is designed to be run by one person working about 12–15 hours a week.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Weekly schedule** — A simple time-table: spend about 5–6 hours reaching out to print shops, 3–4 hours helping people in online communities, and 4–5 hours building pages that show up in Google. Every week, spend 30 minutes checking what is working and what is not.

- **Building your list of target shops (Bet 1)** — Find small print shops, sign shops, and t-shirt shops that still take orders by phone or email. Use Google Maps, Yelp, and Instagram to find them. Build a spreadsheet of 100–200 shops to contact.

- **The personalised video demo (Loom)** — Before you contact a shop, set up a demo of Print-Flow-360 using their own logo and colors. Record a 60-second video showing them their shop's online store. This is the most powerful thing you can send — it is specific to them, not a generic pitch.

- **What to say in emails and messages** — Ready-made email scripts for your first message, a follow-up, and a polite final "should I close the demo?" message. Also includes scripts for Instagram DMs and LinkedIn. Send each one by hand — do not use automation tools at this stage.

- **Joining online communities (Bet 1)** — Pick two places (one Reddit community, one Facebook group for print shops) and answer questions helpfully every day. Only mention your product when it is the honest answer to someone's question. Build trust over weeks, not days.

- **Objection Log** — Every time someone says "no" or pushes back, write down their exact words. After 4 weeks, this list tells you what your home page headline should be and which comparison pages to build first.

- **Comparison pages on Google (Bet 2)** — Write pages like "Print-Flow-360 vs InkSoft" or "Best online ordering software for print shops." These take 9–12 months to rank on Google but keep working forever once they do.

- **Free design tool (Bet 2)** — Let anyone use the design studio for free (no account needed) to make a business card or flyer. When they want to save or order, ask them to sign up. This pulls in visitors and shows off what the product can do.

- **6-week action plan** — A week-by-week checklist from Week 0 (set up your tracker) to Week 6 (review what worked and decide what to do more of).

- **Measuring what works** — Track reply rates, demos booked, and Google rankings. If personal outreach is not getting more than 8–10% positive replies from hand-picked shops, stop and shift effort elsewhere. Do not spend money on ads to fix a message that does not work.

**What to do with this:** Start by building your list of 100–200 shops and recording your first personalised video demo — those two things unlock everything else. Write down every "no" you get in an Objection Log; after four weeks that log will write your website and your Google pages for you.
