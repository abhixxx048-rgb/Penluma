**This document is about the things on a store's website that make shoppers feel safe enough to buy — things like star ratings, security badges, and payment icons. It looks at what Print-Flow-360 stores already have, what is missing or broken, and which fixes will help the most with the least work.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **What "trust signals" are** — Small, visible cues that tell a shopper "this store is real, safe, and worth your money." Examples: star ratings, a padlock icon, accepted payment logos, a money-back-guarantee badge. Without these, many shoppers leave before paying.

- **The good news: the building blocks are already there** — Print-Flow-360 already has star ratings, customer reviews with photos, trust-badge blocks, testimonials, and partner logo sections. The problem is not that these things are missing — it is that most store owners cannot turn them on because they are not connected to the page editor yet.

- **The #1 fix (tiny effort, big result)** — One trust-badge section is fully built and looks great, but store owners cannot add it to their pages because a single config line is missing from the admin editor. Adding that line would instantly unlock it for every store owner.

- **Checkout page is too bare** — At the exact moment a shopper is about to pay, the store only shows a small "SSL Encrypted" label. Best practice is to also show accepted payment icons, a returns/refund note, and a security seal right next to the "Pay" button. This is where the most shoppers leave over safety doubts.

- **Google star ratings could be richer** — The store already tells Google about average star scores, but not about individual reviews. Fixing this can make individual customer reviews show up directly in Google search results — which builds trust before the shopper even clicks.

- **Customer photos and videos in reviews are not showing** — Shoppers already upload photos with their reviews, but the review display blocks only show text and a star rating. Showing those photos is one of the strongest ways to convince a new buyer that the product is real and good.

- **Trust badges should match what the store actually accepts** — If a store does not accept PayPal, the badge should not show PayPal. Badges that lie about payment methods break trust the moment a shopper tries to check out.

**What to do with this:**

First, add the missing config line so store owners can add trust-badge blocks to their pages — it takes almost no effort and unlocks something fully built. Second, add payment icons, a refund note, and a security seal to the checkout page — that is where the most buyers abandon.
