**This document answers one of the hardest questions any small team faces: with limited time and money, where do you focus first? It uses a well-known startup method called the Bullseye Framework to make four big decisions for Print-Flow-360 — who to sell to, how to sell, when to grow, and what to build next. Getting these decisions right prevents the most common failure: trying to do everything at once and making no real progress on anything.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **The Bullseye Framework** — A method for picking where to concentrate your energy. You list every possible option (the outer ring), run cheap, fast tests on the top few (the middle ring), then put almost all your effort into the single thing that is actually working (the inner ring). When it stops working, you repeat the process. The core lesson: concentrate, don't spread thin.

- **ICE Scoring** — A simple scoring tool used alongside Bullseye. For each option, score its expected Impact, your Confidence it will work, and how Easy it is to do (each out of 10). Multiply the three numbers and rank. It turns a gut-feel debate into an objective list.

- **How big is the market, honestly** — About 22,000–28,000 commercial print shops in the US, most with fewer than 5 employees. A previous estimate of 50,000+ was wrong and is corrected here. The good news: even the smaller, correct number is still a finite, manageable list you can build by hand from Google Maps and trade directories.

- **Who to target first (Beachhead segment)** — Small apparel, screen-printing, and sign/promo shops that currently take orders by text or email and have no online store. They feel the pain most sharply, they gather in communities where you can reach them, and Print-Flow-360's design studio and storefront are a natural fit for their products right now.

- **How to sell (GTM motion)** — Let store owners sign up and set themselves up (product-led self-serve), with a 14-day free trial and a short go-live checklist. The founder personally contacts 100–200 shops to land the first customers and learn what objections come up. Do NOT hire a dedicated sales team — at the price Print-Flow-360 charges, the cost of salespeople would never pay back.

- **When to expand to resellers and partners** — Not yet. Open that door only after the current approach (self-serve + founder outreach) stops growing because the reachable pool is used up. Pre-test one regional partner before that happens so there is no gap.

- **Which features to build first** — Fix any known bugs and data-accuracy problems first (the "correctness gate"). Then make the order-to-production spine the single focus: the path from a customer paying to an order being fulfilled and tracked. This chain is currently broken and blocks the whole value the product is supposed to deliver. Fancy differentiators (standing orders, gang-run batching) wait until this works.

- **Four things still to measure** — Whether founder outreach gets enough replies, whether self-serve trials convert at this audience, how much failed-payment recovery dunning emails can achieve (target 50–60% of failed payments recovered), and what exactly will trigger opening the reseller channel.

**What to do with this:**

Fix the foundation first, then fix the order-to-production flow — everything else depends on those two. Start contacting small apparel and sign shops by hand while the self-serve trial is being set up; do not wait until the product is "perfect."
