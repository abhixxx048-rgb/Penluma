**This document is a full review of how your online store looks to customers — every page, every section. A team of design specialists checked 38 pages and scored each area. The verdict: everything works, but the store looks plain and unfinished compared to top print and e-commerce sites like Moo or Vistaprint. The good news is that four fixable problems cause almost all of it, so fixing them lifts the whole site at once.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Why it looks "basic" (the root causes)** — Four things make the store feel flat and cheap: everything is the same visual level with no shadows or depth; headings and body text look almost the same size; most pages ignore the shared style rules and invent their own styles instead; and spacing between sections is random. Fix these four things and every page improves automatically.

- **Page-by-page scores** — Six groups of pages were graded. The style foundation (shared design rules) scored 4 out of 10. The homepage and site header scored 5. The blog, contact, and form pages scored 5. Product browsing and product detail pages scored 6. The cart and checkout scored 6. Login and account pages scored 6. Nothing scored above 6 — all for the same reasons.

- **Shadows and card depth** — Right now almost everything sits on a flat white surface. Professional shops use subtle drop shadows so buttons and cards appear to "lift" off the page. Adding this one effect everywhere makes the site feel modern and trustworthy.

- **Text sizes and label styles** — Headings need to look clearly bigger and bolder than body text, so customers can scan a page easily. Labels on forms ("Name", "Email") are too small and faint right now — they almost disappear.

- **Shared building blocks (design system)** — The store has a good set of shared style rules already written. The problem is that 53 page sections ignore those rules and use their own styles instead, so the site looks like 20 different designs stitched together. Migrating sections to use the shared rules is the single biggest fix.

- **Spacing rhythm** — The gap between sections jumps from small to very large with no consistent pattern. Setting one standard spacing size for all sections makes the page feel calm and professional.

- **Quick wins** — Many improvements take under an hour each: adding drop shadows to cards so they lift on hover, making form labels bigger, improving the hero banner at the top of the homepage, and fixing the blog pages so text is readable.

- **Strategic upgrades (bigger effort)** — Longer work includes building a proper set of reusable components (status badges, product cards, empty-state messages) that all pages share, and making mobile layouts feel as polished as desktop.

- **Page-load feel (logo click lag)** — Clicking the store logo feels slow because the homepage waits for data before showing anything. Fixing this so the page shows a loading skeleton immediately would make the store feel much faster, even before any design changes.

**What to do with this:**

Start with the four root causes — shadows/depth, text sizing, shared style rules, and consistent spacing. These are quick to apply and will make every page look noticeably more professional within days, without redesigning anything from scratch.
