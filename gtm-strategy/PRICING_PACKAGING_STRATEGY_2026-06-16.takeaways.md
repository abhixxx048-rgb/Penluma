**This document decides how Print-Flow-360 should be priced and packaged as a subscription for print-shop owners. It answers three questions: how many plans to offer, what to charge on, and how much to charge. Getting this right matters because pricing too low is just as damaging as pricing too high - and most software companies underprice without realising it.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Three-plan ladder (Good-Better-Best)** - Research shows three plans works best: a cheap starter, a mid-range "hero" that most people should buy, and a premium option that makes the middle feel like great value. Having more than three plans confuses buyers and they often buy nothing. The recommended names are Solo Shop ($39/mo), Growing Shop ($99/mo), and Multi-Location ($249/mo).

- **What you charge on (the "value metric")** - Instead of charging per user seat or taking a cut of sales, the plan price is based on the number of active storefronts/locations. This is easy for a shop owner to understand and predict. It grows naturally as the business grows.

- **Soft order allowance** - Each plan includes a monthly order limit, but hitting that limit never blocks a sale. The shop keeps running. Instead, the owner sees a friendly note saying they are close to their limit and may want to upgrade. No surprise charges, no locked-out store.

- **Four types of print-shop buyers** - Not all print shops are the same. Copy shops and sign shops are the main target (Solo/Growing plans). Commercial printers with business accounts need Multi-Location. Apparel shops are a partial fit. Drop-ship merchandise sellers (like Gelato users) want a free plan with a fee per sale - Print-Flow-360 deliberately does not chase that group.

- **Pricing above competitors on purpose** - Printavo (the closest competitor) charges $244/mo for its mid-tier plan and does not include a storefront, design studio, or B2B accounts. Print-Flow-360 does all of that, so pricing below Printavo's level would signal less value, not more. The Multi-Location plan at $249 sits just above Printavo's hero tier for good reason.

- **Free trial that shows the full product** - New users get 14 days on the Growing Shop level for free, no credit card needed. At the end of the trial, they see all three plan options with a recommendation based on how they actually used the product. If they do not upgrade, the store stays live in a reduced Solo-level mode - they are never locked out completely.

- **Realistic growth target (NRR)** - NRR (net revenue retention) means: does revenue from existing customers grow over time? A target of 100–105% is honest and achievable for a small-business customer base. Chasing 120% is unrealistic and should not be promised.

**What to do with this:**

Talk to 10–20 real print-shop owners to confirm they would pay $39/$99/$249 before building any enforcement. Then build the plan tiers with the soft order nudge - and make sure the upgrade moment shows all three plans clearly, never just one option.
