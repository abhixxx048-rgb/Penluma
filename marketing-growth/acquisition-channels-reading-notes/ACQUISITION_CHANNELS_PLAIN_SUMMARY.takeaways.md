**This document answers one question: how do you get your first print shop customers without spending a lot of money? It picks the two cheapest, most direct ways to reach a small print shop owner and explains why everything else — ads, affiliates, social media — is a waste of time right now. If you follow the plan inside, you could have your first paying shops in 3–6 weeks.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Who your buyer is** — Your ideal customer is a one-person print shop owner who takes orders by phone and has no real website. There are about 50,000 shops like this in the US, and you can find them on Google Maps and Yelp. This is important because you do not need expensive ads to reach them — you can contact them directly, one by one.

- **BET 1: Personal outreach + community trust** — Spend 15 minutes building a sample store with the shop owner's logo, record a short video showing it, and send it to them personally. At the same time, join the online groups where these owners hang out and be genuinely helpful for several weeks before mentioning your product. This combination is fast, nearly free, and gets real replies.

- **BET 2: Google pages + a free design tool** — Write a handful of web pages comparing your product to competitors ("InkSoft alternatives," etc.) and build a free design tool that anyone can use. These take 9–12 months to show up on Google, but once they do, they bring in customers while you sleep — for years, for free.

- **The Bullseye method** — A simple decision tool: list every possible way to get customers, pick the top 3 to test cheaply, then put all your energy into whichever one actually works. It stops you from spreading yourself too thin.

- **8 things NOT to do** — Do not run Google ads (too expensive for the return), do not turn outreach into automated cold email (it kills the personal touch), do not chase big company deals before you have happy customers, and do not quit on SEO after 3 months (it needs 9–12 months to work).

**What to do with this:** Start this week — build a list of local print shops from Google Maps, make 3–5 personal demo videos, and join the two communities named in the document. That is the fastest path to your first paying customer.
