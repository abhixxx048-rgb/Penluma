**This document answers one question: who should Print-Flow-360 sell to first, and in what order should it grow after that? It uses a well-known business framework (called "Crossing the Chasm") to make this decision carefully, backed by real research on competitors and market size. Getting this choice right will decide whether the platform survives its first two years.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Pick one type of customer first ("the beachhead")** - Instead of trying to sell to every print shop at once, the plan is to win one specific type of customer completely before moving on. This is like planting a flag on one beach before pushing further inland. It builds trust and word-of-mouth.

- **Who to target first: print shops that use no software at all** - Small general print shops (business cards, flyers, banners, brochures) that still take orders by phone, email, or spreadsheet. These shops are underserved - no big competitor is fighting for them - and our product fits their needs better than any rival today.

- **Why NOT to start with custom T-shirt / team-wear shops** - Every major competitor (Printavo, InkSoft, DecoNetwork, OrderMyGear) already has a mature, well-loved "group store" feature for team and school orders. Entering that market first means competing on the one thing we don't have yet. That is a losing fight.

- **What still needs to be built (the "whole product")** - Even for the first group of customers, three things must be finished before a real shop will trust the platform: (1) a basic file-quality check so customers can't upload bad print files, (2) a digital proof-and-approval step, and (3) correct tax handling including exemptions. Shipping labels and split orders can wait until later customers need them.

- **Expansion order (the "bowling pins")** - After winning the first group, grow in this order: (2) the same shops that now want corporate/business clients, (3) small print chains and franchises, (4) then - and only then - apparel and team-wear stores (once the group-store feature is properly built), (5) then promotional-products sellers.

- **The $100M reality check** - The first target group alone will never reach $100M in revenue. The big number only works if the expansion into groups 2–5 is also successful. The plan is honest about this so nobody is misled.

**What to do with this:** Focus the first sales effort entirely on print shops that currently have no ordering software - reach them through direct outreach and print-community forums. Before opening up to the next customer type, collect at least 10–20 happy reference shops from this first group. Do not start building the team-store feature until it is fully funded and scheduled as a real project.
