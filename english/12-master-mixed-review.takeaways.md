**This is the final practice session of the Developer English course. It mixes every grammar rule from all previous lessons into one big exercise so you can check what you have learned and what you still need to fix. It matters because writing clean, professional English in commits, messages, and reports makes your work easier for your team to understand and trust.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Quick reminder table** — A one-page cheat sheet showing every rule covered in the course and the single most important thing to remember for each one. Keep it beside you while you do the exercises.
- **Part A — Fix the sentence** — Ten real-looking sentences that developers actually write, each with one or more mistakes. You rewrite them correctly. This trains your fingers, not just your brain.
- **Part B — Fill the blank** — Ten sentences where one word is missing. You pick the right word from two or three choices. Focuses on the trickiest pairs: much/many, its/it's, your/you're, then/than.
- **Part C — Choose A or B** — Four pairs of sentences where only one is correct. Tests possessives, subject-verb agreement, tenses, and spelling.
- **Part D — Clean up the messy dev message** — Four badly written real-world messages (a Slack standup, a commit message, a PR description, and the very first sentence that started this course). You rewrite each one so a teammate can read it without effort.
- **Answer key with explanations** — Every answer is given with a one-line reason so you understand *why*, not just *what* is correct.

**What to do with this:**

Work through all 28 exercises with a pen or in a text file before you look at the answers. After checking, note which sections you got wrong most, then go back and re-read that lesson. The three habits that will improve your writing the fastest: always end a sentence with a full stop, always use a capital **I**, and before writing "much" or "many" ask yourself "can I count it?" — if yes, use **many** and add an **-s** to the noun.
