**This document is a health check of 13 features already in the product. A team of research agents read the real code, found where things go quietly wrong for store owners, and ranked what to fix first. It matters because several features are currently lying to owners - the screen says "Saved" or "Success" while the real work silently did not happen.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **The biggest problem: success messages that lie** - In several places, an owner does something (saves a price, creates a product, sends an email), sees a green "Success" message, and walks away - but nothing actually happened behind the scenes. The owner can't tell, has no error to look at, and often only notices later when money is missing or a customer complains. This is called "confirmed-then-discarded" and it is the top priority to fix.

- **Two verified bugs (checked line by line in the code)** - (1) The "Markup" profit field on a product looks like it saves - the green preview box even updates - but the value is silently thrown away every time. The product keeps selling at cost. (2) The AI Product Builder says "Product created successfully" with a tick and a confidence score, but the product is never attached to the owner's shop, so it never appears in the store. Re-running the prompt piles up invisible duplicates.

- **Six patterns that repeat across the whole product** - The same kinds of mistakes appear in many different features: success messages that are not true; blank screens that look broken when they are just empty; developer-only words showing up where plain English belongs; destructive actions (like sending an email blast or cancelling a job) with no warning about what will be lost; third-party connections (payment gateways, email providers) that accept wrong credentials without any test; and two separate tools doing the same job when one would be simpler.

- **A four-wave fix plan** - Wave 1 is the fastest: fix the phantom Markup field, make "Mark as Paid" actually send the receipt, fix the AI Builder product assignment, and add a recipient-count confirmation before bulk emails go out. Wave 2 builds small shared helpers (plain labels, empty/error states, confirm modals, integration test buttons) that improve every screen at once. Wave 3 tackles bigger work: real onboarding, a working pricing tester, and a friendly email editor. Wave 4 removes duplicate tools.

- **Per-feature findings** - Every one of the 13 modules (Products, Pricing, Orders, Designer, B2B, Storefront Builder, AI Builder, Action Center, Integrations, Payment Gateways, Onboarding, Analytics, Email/SMS) has a single sharpest pain point identified and a ranked list of fixes ready to pick up.

**What to do with this:** Start with Wave 1 - five small fixes that stop the product from lying to owners. Then build the four shared building blocks in Wave 2, which repair dozens of screens in one go. Treat "the success message must be true" as a rule that every new feature must pass before it ships.
