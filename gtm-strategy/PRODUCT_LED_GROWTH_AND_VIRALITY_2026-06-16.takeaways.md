**This document is a research report about how to grow Print-Flow-360 by letting the product itself bring in new customers — instead of relying on ads or a sales team. It covers how to design the free trial so more owners actually pay, how to get the platform's name in front of new customers for free using tools that already exist, and how to make sure new store owners reach the moment where they go "this actually works" as fast as possible. These are decisions for the platform team, but they directly shape how rewarding the platform feels to use.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** — A way of growing where the product itself does the selling. Instead of paying for ads or hiring salespeople, you make the product so easy and valuable that owners sign up, set up, and upgrade on their own.

- **Reverse trial** — Start every new store owner on the full paid plan for 14 days, at no charge and no card required. At the end of 14 days, if they haven't paid, they don't get locked out — they move to a limited state. Research shows this converts about 5–6 times better than a traditional free plan.

- **Dormant/parking tier** — Instead of cutting off access entirely when a trial ends, keep the owner's designs, products, and pricing rules safe but take the public store offline. This costs almost nothing to run and gives the owner a real reason to come back and pay ("your store is saved, go live with one click").

- **"Powered by" attribution loop** — Adding a small, tasteful "Built with Print-Flow-360" link to every store footer, proof approval email, and designer export. This is free advertising on surfaces the platform already runs. Today, 100% of this opportunity is unused. Removing the badge becomes a clear reason to upgrade to a paid plan.

- **Activation and the "aha moment"** — The moment an owner truly believes the platform works is when a real customer places a real order. The current setup guide stops at "preview your store," which is too early. Extending it to include a first real order — with a genuine celebration — is the single biggest activation improvement available.

- **Freemium economics** — Why a permanent, always-on free plan is the wrong choice here. Every live store costs real money to run (hosting, storage, file processing). A free live store that never pays cannibalizes the cheapest paid plan and is expensive to carry. The dormant tier solves this: data is alive, costs are near zero.

- **Growth experiments** — How to test ideas cheaply when you don't have millions of visitors. The key tool is a simple "fake door" test: show a button for a feature, measure how many people click it, before building it. Run a short weekly meeting to score and prioritize ideas. Never run a classic A/B split test on the main funnel — you don't have enough traffic yet.

- **Sequencing** — The right order to build things matters. Measure the funnel first (you currently can't measure how many owners reach their first order). Then add the "Powered by" badge and improve the setup guide. Then change the trial model and build the dormant tier. Skipping steps creates expensive bugs.

**What to do with this:** Ship the "Powered by Print-Flow-360" footer link on every store now — it costs nothing and starts working immediately. Then focus on measuring how many new owners actually reach their first real order, because until that number exists, nothing else can be improved with confidence.
