**This document explains how to have short phone calls with store owners who signed up, walked away, or cancelled - and how to get the real reason, not just the polite one. It matters because if you build the next feature based on a guess about why people leave, you'll almost always guess wrong. Talking to even a few real people each week gives you better answers than any survey.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Four groups to talk to** - Most founders only track who signed up and who said no. This document says you need four groups: people who joined and got their store live (wins), people who looked but chose another tool (lost to a competitor), people who looked but did nothing (lost because they didn't decide), and people who signed up but never set up their store (the "silent leak"). That last group is usually the biggest problem.

- **The 14-day rule** - Talk to someone within 7–14 days of them cancelling or going quiet. After about two weeks, people forget the real reason and just say "it was too expensive" or "I didn't have time." Those short answers are almost always wrong.

- **The founder should make the calls** - You don't have a sales team yet, so you are the neutral person. Shop owners will be much more honest with "the founder who wants to learn" than with a survey form. The only rule: listen, don't defend the product.

- **Laddering - asking "why" several times** - When someone says "it was too expensive," that is almost never the true reason. You ask what they mean, then what that means, then what that meant for them - about five questions deep. Research shows the real reason usually only comes out after four or five follow-up questions. A worked example in this doc shows how "too expensive" turned into "I never finished loading my products so I never saw it working."

- **Ready-made interview scripts** - The document gives you word-for-word questions for each of the four groups. Each call is 20–30 minutes. You can copy the questions straight into a document and follow along.

- **Tagging what you hear** - After each call, give it a short label (like "never got store live" or "missing a feature"). Over time, counting those labels tells you whether your biggest problem is the product, the setup process, the price, or something else.

- **The cancellation pop-up survey** - You should add a one-question survey when someone cancels. It catches everyone, but the answers are not very accurate - people click fast. Its real job is to find who to invite for a proper call.

- **Email templates to get people on a call** - The document gives short, honest email drafts for each group. Offer a small gift card ($75). The "founder genuinely wants to understand" angle works better than any sales script.

**What to do with this:**

This week, pull a list of everyone who signed up in the last four weeks and tag them as won, lost, churned, or never set up. Then send five emails to people who signed up but never got their store live - that is almost certainly your biggest fixable leak. Book one 20-minute call with a real person and record it. One honest conversation will teach you more than reading any research document.
