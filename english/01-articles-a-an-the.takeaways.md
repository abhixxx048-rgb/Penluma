**This document teaches you when to write "a", "an", "the", or nothing before a word in English. Getting this right makes your messages, pull requests, and comments sound natural and professional - not like a log file.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **The three choices (a / an / the / nothing)** - "a" or "an" means one of many things, or the first time you mention something. "the" means a specific thing both you and the reader already know. No article at all is for general ideas or uncountable things like "data", "code", or "feedback".

- **Sound, not spelling** - You pick "a" or "an" based on how the word *sounds*, not how it is spelled. "an SQL query" (because SQL sounds like "ess"), "a URL" (because URL sounds like "you-arr-ell"), "an honest mistake" (because the "h" is silent - it sounds like "onnest").

- **Why developers drop articles** - When writing code, commit messages, or quick messages, people skip small words to go faster. This is a habit that carries into real sentences and makes them sound robotic or incomplete.

- **When "the" is wrong** - Do not use "the" with uncountable words ("the data", "the feedback", "the code") or with tool and product names ("the GitHub", "the Slack", "the Redis"). Use "the" only when you mean one specific known thing.

- **Commit messages are the exception** - In a commit message, dropping articles is accepted style (`fix null check in cart service` is fine). But in PR descriptions, Slack messages, and code comments, write full sentences with articles.

- **Common mistakes fixed** - The document shows a table of real examples, like "open pull request" → "open **a** pull request", and "send the feedback in the slack" → "send **feedback** in **Slack**", with a clear reason for each fix.

- **Practice drills** - Twelve sentences to fix or fill in, with a full answer key explaining every choice. Good for building the habit through repetition.

**What to do with this:** Use the quick rule - *specific and known → the; first mention or one of many → a/an; general or uncountable → nothing* - whenever you write a Slack message, PR description, or code comment.
