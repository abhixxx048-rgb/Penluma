**This document ties together five separate research reports into one clear plan for how Print-Flow-360 should find its first customers, price its product, and build a business that is hard to copy. It is the master summary that connects all the strategy work done in June 2026. If you only read one strategy document, read this one first.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Who to sell to first (Beachhead)** - Focus on small print shops that have NO online ordering software at all. These shops are ignored by big competitors, and our product already does most of what they need. Do NOT try to win the clothing-decoration (apparel) market first - that space has strong, well-known rivals and we are missing key features for it.

- **How to find customers (Bullseye channels)** - Do not spend money on ads. Instead, the founder reaches out personally and shows up in two print-industry online communities. That is it. Concentrate all effort there; do not spread thin across many channels.

- **Pricing (Good-Better-Best tiers)** - Three clear plans: Solo at $39/month, Growing at $99/month (the main one to push), and Multi-Location at $249/month. Charge per storefront. Never block a sale just because a shop is over a usage limit - instead, gently nudge them to upgrade. Aim for customers to spend a little more each year, not less.

- **Trial offer (Reverse trial)** - Let new sign-ups try the full product free for 14 days with no credit card needed. This brings in 3–4 times more sign-ups than requiring a card upfront. Only show the plan-choice screen when they are ready to pay.

- **Self-serve vs. personal selling (GTM motion)** - Smaller shops should be able to sign up and set up on their own (no sales team needed). Larger shops or businesses get personal help from the founder. Hiring salespeople does not make financial sense at these price levels.

- **The core product to finish first (Order-to-production spine)** - Before growing, fix the end-to-end order flow: proof approval, basic file checking (preflight), correct tax calculation, then shipping labels and tracking. This is the single most important thing to build - every part of the strategy depends on it.

- **How to make the business hard to leave (Moat)** - The more a shop configures their store - products, pricing rules, templates, customer accounts - the harder it is for them to move to a competitor. That is the main protection. Later, handling payments directly (earning a fee on every sale) makes it even stickier.

- **A critical unknown to test first** - Can a non-technical shop owner actually set up the store on their own, without help? This must be tested with 10–20 real owners before spending money to grow. If they cannot do it alone, the whole self-serve plan needs rethinking.

**What to do with this:**

First, make sure the product works correctly end-to-end and fix any serious payment/security issues - nothing else moves until that is done. Then test whether real, non-technical shop owners can go live on their own. Only after those two gates pass should you invest in pricing tiers, growth channels, or new features.
