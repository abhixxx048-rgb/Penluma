**This document checks whether the platform is ready to hear from customers — and the answer is: not yet.** Right now the shop can take orders and payments, but it has no way to ask buyers "were you happy?", no way to catch when a checkout breaks, and no way to tell the store owner when something goes wrong. **If problems appear, they will pile up silently until customers stop coming back — which is the worst way to find out.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Post-delivery satisfaction survey (blocker)** — After a customer gets their printed order, there is no automatic "How did we do?" email. The founder has no tool to find out if the product is actually working for real buyers. This is the single most important thing to fix before launch.

- **Checkout and payment error tracking** — When a customer's payment fails or checkout breaks, nothing records it. So the store owner sees "people abandoned their cart" but cannot tell if they left on purpose or because something broke. The fix is to track those errors separately so the real cause is visible.

- **Abandoned cart recovery** — When a customer fills a cart but never buys, there is no follow-up email to bring them back. For a print store where customers put real design effort into their cart, this is direct lost revenue.

- **Owner health dashboard** — The analytics page shows sales numbers but cannot answer "Why did people not finish checkout?" or "Are customers complaining about print quality in reviews?" A new "Friction and Health" section is needed so the owner can spot problems without digging through logs.

- **Review and message notifications** — Customers can write product reviews and send order messages, but the store owner is never notified. A complaint about a bad print job, or a one-star review, can sit unread for weeks.

- **Error alerts** — When something breaks on the site (server errors, PDF failures), the owner only sees a wall of technical code they cannot read. No plain-English alert is sent, and errors are never grouped or summarized.

**What to do with this:** Fix the post-delivery "How did we do?" email first — without it, launch is flying blind. Then make sure the owner gets notified when a customer leaves a review or sends a message. Everything else can follow after those two are live.
