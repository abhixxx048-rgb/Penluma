**This document is a practical growth plan for Print-Flow-360 as a business — covering how to set subscription prices, stop customers from leaving, and bring in new ones through word-of-mouth. It is written for the people building and running the platform, not for print-shop owners themselves. The goal is to grow a healthy, loyal base of paying print-shop subscribers without spending a lot on advertising.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **How to price our plans** — Charge print shops based on how many store locations they run, not based on how many staff they have or how much revenue they make. Offer three clearly named plans (something like "Solo Shop / Growing Shop / Multi-Location") so most shops naturally pick the middle one. Offer a discount of roughly two months free for shops that pay yearly instead of monthly.

- **Free trial design** — Give new shops 14 days to try the platform for free. The goal during those 14 days is to help them get their first product live and take their first order — that moment is the thing that makes them want to stay and pay.

- **Stopping accidental cancellations (dunning)** — Around 20–40% of cancellations happen not because a shop wants to leave, but because their card payment failed. Sending a plain, friendly reminder ("Your card was declined — update it here to keep your store online") can recover roughly 40–60% of those shops. This is the cheapest win available.

- **Keeping shops from leaving (retention)** — Most shops that cancel do so in the first 90 days. Getting a shop to publish their first product and receive a real order quickly is the single biggest thing that keeps them around long-term.

- **Referral program** — Build a simple invite system: a shop invites another shop owner, and both get a free month when the new shop pays their first bill. This costs almost nothing and brings in higher-quality customers than any ad.

- **Customer reviews and real stories** — Shop owners trust other shop owners more than they trust software companies. Collecting real, named stories ("Smith Signs cut quote time from 2 days to 10 minutes") and getting reviews on software listing sites builds the trust that turns a hesitant owner into a paying customer.

- **Lifecycle emails** — Emails that fire automatically when something specific happens (a shop goes quiet, a payment fails, a trial is about to end) work roughly 10 times better than sending the same email to everyone at once. The platform already has the email infrastructure — it just needs these automatic trigger-based sequences built on top.

- **A health score for at-risk shops** — Track simple signals (has the shop logged in recently? published a product? received an order?) to spot shops that are drifting away 60–90 days before they cancel, then send a targeted message to bring them back.

- **Phased build order** — The document lays out what to build first (measuring basics, then fixing failed payments, then onboarding, then referrals and reviews) so effort goes to the highest-impact work before anything else.

**What to do with this:** Fix failed-payment recovery first — it is the fastest, cheapest way to stop losing shops that never intended to leave. Then focus on getting every new shop to publish their first product and receive their first order within their first two weeks, because that one milestone determines whether they stay or go.
