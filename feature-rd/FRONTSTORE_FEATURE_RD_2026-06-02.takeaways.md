**This document is a detailed check-up of your customer-facing online store — the pages your shoppers actually see and use. It matters because a team of reviewers read through the real store code AND walked through the live store to find exactly where shoppers get confused, misled, or give up before buying.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **The biggest problem — buttons that lie** — Several important buttons (like "Request a Quote" and "Add to Cart" on category pages) look like they work, but do nothing. A shopper clicks, sees a success message, and nothing actually happened. No quote was sent. No item was added. This is the single most damaging thing in the store because shoppers who feel tricked stop trusting everything else.

- **Fake data shown as real** — The store shows made-up things as if they are real: a fake order number generated by the computer, a "2,847 Orders This Week" stat that is invented, a "Diwali offer" banner that appears even on stores that never set one up, and a whole profile page showing "John Doe" with fake orders. Shoppers who spot this leave.

- **Silent failures — the store goes blank instead of explaining** — When something goes wrong (a page fails to load, an address fails to save), the store often shows nothing — a blank space, an endless spinner, or a misleading "no results" message. Shoppers think the store is broken.

- **Disabled buttons with no explanation** — Buttons like "Add to Cart" or "Place Order" are sometimes greyed out with no message telling the shopper why or what to do next. This leaves people stuck with no way forward.

- **Login suddenly required at the worst moment** — Shoppers who are ready to buy get sent to a login screen without warning. This interrupts the purchase at the highest-stakes point.

- **Wrong or mixed-up prices** — Some pages show prices in dollars ($) and others in rupees (₹) regardless of the store's actual currency setting. The cart total sometimes includes tax without showing a tax line, so the total appears higher than the subtotal for no visible reason.

- **Deleting things with no warning** — Actions like "Clear Cart" happen instantly with no "are you sure?" message. Shoppers lose their cart by accident with no way to undo it.

- **Broken on phones** — Key parts of the store overflow, overlap, or are too small to tap on a standard phone screen (375px wide), which is how most shoppers visit.

- **Logins being silently rejected** — On stores using the "I'm not a robot" security check, a bug causes every login attempt to fail even when the shopper does everything right. This blocks purchases entirely.

- **Checkout can claim success when no payment happened** — In some situations the store shows "Order Placed Successfully!" and clears the cart before any payment was actually confirmed. The shopper thinks they ordered; no order exists.

**What to do with this:**

Fix the three purchase blockers first — they stop sales today: (1) logins silently failing on stores with the robot-check enabled (a few hours of work), (2) "Add to Cart" and "Request a Quote" buttons that do nothing (wire them to the real backend or hide them until they work), and (3) the checkout showing a success screen before payment is verified (show a neutral "Confirming your payment…" screen instead). Everything else in the ranked list follows in order of impact.
