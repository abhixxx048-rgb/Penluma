**This document explains how to turn website visitors into paying customers for a print shop software business. It covers everything from how the website should look, to how the free trial should work, to how new customers get set up — and why getting people to their first win quickly is the single most important thing to focus on.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **The 5-stage growth map** — A simple way to find where potential customers are dropping off. Are they visiting but not signing up? Signing up but not using the product? Using it but not paying? The rule is: find the biggest leak and fix that first, not everything at once.

- **Getting people to their first win fast** — The single biggest factor in whether someone becomes a paying customer is how quickly they see the product working for them. For this software, that winning moment is: their online store goes live and they receive their first order. Everything else in this document serves that goal.

- **The first page visitors see (landing page)** — The page needs one clear headline that says what the product does in plain words, one button to start, and a real screenshot of the product in action. No credit card should be needed to begin. Keep the sign-up form short — just an email address.

- **How the free trial should work** — The best approach is a 14-day free trial with no credit card required at the start. At the end of 14 days, the store stays open in a limited way rather than being locked out — the owner keeps something to come back to. Ask for payment right after they place their first order, not on a fixed calendar day.

- **Getting new customers set up (onboarding)** — New customers should see a short 5-step checklist that walks them through going live: add a product, set a price, publish the store, share the link. The dashboard should never be blank — pre-fill a sample product so it doesn't look broken. Celebrate each milestone with a plain message like "Your store is live!"

- **Pricing page** — Show three clear plans with real prices visible. Highlight the middle plan as "Most Popular." Offer "2 months free" for paying yearly. No technical jargon, no hidden prices.

- **What to measure** — Track how many new sign-ups go live and receive their first order within 7 days. That one number is the main health signal for the whole business.

**What to do with this:** Start by setting up tracking so you can measure how many new customers go live and get their first order within 7 days — you cannot improve what you cannot see. Then make sure every new sign-up sees a pre-filled demo store, not an empty screen, because a blank dashboard feels broken and people leave before giving it a chance.
