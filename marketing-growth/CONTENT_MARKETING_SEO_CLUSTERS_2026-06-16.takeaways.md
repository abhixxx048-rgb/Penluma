**This document is a plan for getting more people to find your store through Google — and then actually buy something once they arrive. Right now the blog exists but it is not connected to your products, so visitors read an article and leave without buying. This plan fixes that.**

**The main parts explained simply:**

- **Hub-and-spoke blog structure** — Instead of a flat list of blog posts, you organise them like a wheel. One big "pillar" article covers a broad topic (e.g. "Business Cards Guide"). Smaller "cluster" articles cover the details (e.g. "Matte vs Gloss", "Which paper weight?"). They all link to each other. Google rewards this because it shows you really know your subject.

- **TOFU / MOFU / BOFU** — These are three types of shopper. TOFU (top of funnel) is someone just browsing ideas. MOFU (middle) is comparing options. BOFU (bottom) is ready to buy. The plan maps your blog content to each type so every article nudges readers toward a purchase.

- **"Shop this guide" block** — A simple box at the bottom of a blog post that says "Ready to order? Here are the products from this guide." This turns readers into buyers. It is the single most important thing to build because the blog traffic already exists but nothing converts it to sales.

- **Schema markup (structured data)** — Hidden code that tells Google exactly what your page is about, so it can show star ratings, FAQ answers, and breadcrumbs directly in search results. The code templates already exist in the store but are not switched on yet.

- **Sitemap** — A list of every page on your store sent to Google so it can find and index them all. The current sitemap is a basic single file; the plan upgrades it to handle large stores properly.

- **SaaS marketing site** — A completely separate website for Print-Flow-360 itself (not your store) to attract print shop owners who are looking for software. This does not exist yet and is a longer-term project.

- **Phased roadmap** — The work is split into three stages: first connect blog articles to products (quick, high return), then improve Google signals like schema and sitemap, then build the Print-Flow-360 marketing site.

**What to do with this:**

First, link your blog articles to the products they talk about — this is the fastest way to turn existing readers into paying customers. Second, organise your blog posts into pillar-and-cluster groups so Google sees you as an authority in your niche.
